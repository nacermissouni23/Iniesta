## What I do

Provide the qualitative context essential for fair player comparisons:

- Career timeline mapping: clubs, leagues, managers, key teammates
- Tactical role definition: position, responsibilities, freedom within system
- Era context: rules (backpass, offside, tackle from behind), tactical trends, league competitiveness
- Key moments: finals, decisive matches, iconic performances
- Trophy and award catalog with context (strength of competition, role in winning)
- Playing style breakdown: technical strengths/weaknesses, physical profile, football intelligence
- Leadership and intangibles: captaincy, influence on teammates, big-game temperament

## Key player profiles for this project

### Andrés Iniesta (born 1984)
- **Position**: Central midfielder / interior (left-8 in 4-3-3), occasionally false winger
- **Club career**: Barcelona (2002-2018, La Masia product), Vissel Kobe (2018-2023), Emirates Club (2023-2024)
- **Style**: Elite close control in tight spaces, 90% career pass completion, "the ghost" who makes the team tick, dribbling under pressure, line-breaking passes
- **Peak system**: Guardiola's 4-3-3 tiki-taka, high pressing, positional play
- **Iconic moments**: 2009 UCL semi vs Chelsea (stoppage-time equalizer), 2010 World Cup final (winning goal), Euro 2012 (Player of the Tournament)
- **Honours**: 9 La Liga, 4 UCL, 3 Club World Cup, 1 World Cup, 2 Euros, UEFA Men's Player of the Year 2012
- **Tactical context**: Played in the most dominant club side in history, surrounded by all-time greats (Messi, Xavi, Busquets)

### Zinedine Zidane (born 1972)
- **Position**: Attacking midfielder / enganche (playmaker behind strikers), occasionally left midfield
- **Club career**: Cannes (1989-1992), Bordeaux (1992-1996), Juventus (1996-2001), Real Madrid (2001-2006)
- **Style**: Graceful on the ball, exceptional first touch, both-footed, powerful volleys, heading ability (1.85m), vision for decisive passes, big-game performer
- **Peak system**: 90s Serie A catenaccio (Juventus), Galacticos era (Real Madrid), 4-4-1-1 or 4-2-3-1
- **Iconic moments**: 1998 World Cup final (2 goals vs Brazil), 2002 UCL final (volley vs Leverkusen), 2006 World Cup final (Panenka penalty)
- **Honours**: 1 World Cup, 1 Euro, 1 UCL, 2 Serie A, 1 La Liga, Ballon d'Or 1998, 3x FIFA World Player of the Year
- **Tactical context**: Played across three very different leagues and styles; adapted from 90s defensive football to 2000s attacking freedom

## Era comparison notes for fair analysis

- Zidane played in the pre-analytics era (1989-2006); detailed event data is sparse before ~1999
- Iniesta played in the analytics era (2002-2024); comprehensive data exists for almost his entire career
- Rule differences: backpass ban (1992), tackle from behind ban (1994), offside tweaks — all favored the creative player progressively
- Tactical evolution: Zidane faced Catenaccio and man-marking; Iniesta faced zonal pressing and sophisticated defensive blocks
- Iniesta's Barca had ~65% average possession; Zidane's Juventus had ~50-55%, his Madrid had ~55-60%
- These differences must be acknowledged when comparing raw stats

## When to use me

Use this skill whenever a statistical comparison needs qualitative context — to adjust for era differences, explain stat disparities through tactical roles, and ensure the comparison is intellectually honest about what "better" means.
