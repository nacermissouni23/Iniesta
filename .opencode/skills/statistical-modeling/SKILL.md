## What I do

Model football phenomena using mathematical and statistical frameworks:

- Poisson distribution: model goal scoring as independent events, predict match outcomes, identify over/underperformance
- Passing networks: build passing graphs to reveal team structure, triangles, and preferred combinations
- Flow fields: model player movement as vector systems for pressing and attacking patterns
- Extreme value analysis: identify outlier performances (Messi, Ronaldo, Zidane, Iniesta as statistical extremes)
- Pitch control / Voronoi: model space occupation and pass probability
- Markov chains: assign credit through build-up sequences (xG chain)
- Clustering: find player similarity groups for comparison and scouting
- Logistic regression: build shot probability models (distance, angle, defender proximity)

## Key findings from Soccermatics

- Goals follow a Poisson distribution — individual goals are random, long-run rates are stable
- Barcelona's 2010-11 passing network formed perfect geometric triangles with options in all directions
- Defensive flow fields show optimal pressing angles — intercept passing lanes, not the ball
- Ibrahimovic's wonder goals follow Newtonian physics — few angle/speed combos produce goals
- Kelly criterion optimizes betting stake size given estimated probabilities
- Groups outperform individuals for predictions when members act independently
- Crowd noise follows the same mathematics as bird flocking and cicada choruses

## When to use me

Use this skill when building predictive models, analyzing player similarity, constructing passing networks, modeling goal randomness, or needing statistical rigor for player comparison studies like Iniesta vs Zidane.
